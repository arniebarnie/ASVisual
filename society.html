The Impact of Automobiles on People's Lifestyles

The mass adoption of the automobile has had a major impact on the social fabric and culture of the general public.

As people left cities and moved into suburbs, the social environment in which they lived in also profoundly changed. According to the experiences of Andrew Price, who has lived in both cities and suburbs, there are major differences in the environments of these places. When he lived in a city, “[he] used to have random encounters with strangers, often daily. These were usually nothing more than simple interruptions. The elderly lady that asks for help at the train station … so these random encounters played an important part in developing [his] social skills and feeling comfortable around strangers” (Price) and while living in the suburbs, “[he] eliminated most of these random encounters” (Price). The social interactions between people in cities and suburbs is quite different and the distance between people in suburbs has caused a large amount of isolation.

Women's Freedom

The role of women in society was also profoundly impacted by the mass adoption of the car. “The urban housewife of 1925 who could do well without a car was transformed into the the suburban housewife of 1960 who required a car to do all of her shopping or to pick up the kids after school” (Hess 9). With the use of cars by women, they were no longer limited to the house and could escape their normal jobs at home and go into the “cultural scene” (Hess 9). Now, women’s work was no longer limited to domestic duties and they could enter the male-dominated job market. In this regard, women achieved new levels of equality with men thanks to the automobile.
